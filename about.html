Our Approach

Real psychology. Real warmth. No gimmicks.

We started The Extra Ordinary Life because young people deserve a place to grow that's both genuinely safe and genuinely human. Here's what we believe, how we work, and who you'll be in a room with.

What we believe

You're not a problem to be solved. You're a person to be understood.

So much of what's aimed at young people starts from what's "wrong" with them. We start from the opposite belief: that given safety, honesty and the right tools, people grow themselves. Our job isn't to fix you — it's to hold a space steady enough that you can.

Everything we do is rooted in established psychology — attachment, emotional regulation, self-determination theory, and the science of how people actually change. We just deliver it the way a good friend would: warmly, and without the jargon.

Our principles

The non-negotiables behind every session

Safety before everything

Psychological safety isn't a nice-to-have — it's the whole foundation. Nothing happens until the room is safe.

Invitation, never pressure

Growth that's forced isn't growth. Everything is opt-in, and your "no" is always respected instantly.

Evidence, not hype

We use approaches with real grounding in psychology — and we're honest about what these sessions can and can't do.

Radical honesty about money

We're transparent on cost, and we hold funded places so nobody is excluded by their bank balance.

Belonging over performance

You don't have to earn your place by being impressive. You belong here as you are.

Take it into the world

The sessions matter because of Monday. Everything points toward a life you actually live.

For parents & carers

Our promise to the people who love them.

Trusting an organisation with someone you care about is a big deal. We don't take it lightly, and we'd never ask you to take us on faith. Here's our commitment to you.

We're an open book.

Ask for our safeguarding policy, insurance, facilitator qualifications or venue details — we'll send them gladly.

We'll talk to you directly.

Want a real conversation before they go? Book a call with a facilitator. No question is too small.

Consent is built in.

For under-18s, a parent or guardian's informed consent is part of signing up — never an afterthought.

We know our limits.

We're personal growth, not therapy. If your young person needs clinical support, we'll say so and help you find it.

They're never alone.

With a 1:6 ratio and a dedicated wellbeing lead, someone is always paying attention to how each person is doing.

You'll hear from us if needed.

If anything concerning arises during a session, we follow clear safeguarding steps — and we'll contact you.
